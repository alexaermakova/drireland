You are not failing. Your body is changing.

Perimenopause can begin years before menopause itself, sometimes in the late 30s or 40s. Hormonal shifts can affect metabolism, sleep, stress response, mood, cognition, and weight regulation long before periods fully stop.

Many women are told their symptoms are normal, stress related, or simply part of aging. Others receive fragmented care that treats symptoms one by one without looking at the larger pattern.

Dr. Ireland’s practice focuses on understanding that pattern, reviewing labs carefully, and creating a plan that supports both current quality of life and long term health.

How care works

A structured way to understand what is happening.

Care begins with careful listening, medical history, and lab review. From there, Dr. Ireland helps identify patterns and create a plan that fits your goals, timeline, and budget.

Ongoing Support

Treatment can be adjusted over time based on symptoms, lab results, safety, and your comfort level.

Questions

Common questions before starting care.

A first visit should not feel like an exam you can fail. These questions are here to make the process clearer before you decide what kind of support you need.

Do I need labs before my first visit?

No. Existing labs are helpful, but additional testing can be ordered if needed.

Can you work with Kaiser labs and records?

Yes. Existing labs and records from Kaiser and other providers can often be incorporated into care planning.

Do you prescribe hormone therapy?

Hormone therapy may be discussed when appropriate for your symptoms, goals, and medical history.

What if I am not sure hormones are right for me?

Treatment decisions are collaborative. Recommendations are discussed carefully before starting anything new.

Is this covered by insurance?

Dr. Ireland operates as an independent private practice and does not bill insurance directly.

How long does treatment usually take?

Some women benefit from a single consultation. Others prefer ongoing support and monitoring over time.
